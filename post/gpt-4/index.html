GPT-4 Release

Mar 15, 2023
3 min read
AI

What is going on

Open AI have recently announced the release of GPT-4, the successor of GPT-3.5. It is a huge jump from its predecessor, and is continuing the evolution of Artificial Intelligence and its capabilities. The best thing about it, is that it is multimodal.

What does ‘multimodal’ mean?

The GPT-4 AI model is multimodal, meaning it can take in both image and text inputs, emitting text outputs. This is a massive game-changer, as you can simply give an image input, ask a question about it, and it will translate that image into something it can understand, process it, then give you back the answer to the question. People that used GPT-3.5 to write out long essays for them can now simply take a photo of a maths problem and tell ChatGPT (which GPT-4 is being integrated into) to answer their maths question without having to do anything else.

Features

Now you are probably wondering, what is the difference between GPT-3.5 and GPT-4? Well, the answer is a lot. As stated on the GPT-4 Research Page,

In a casual conversation, the distinction 
between GPT-3.5 and GPT-4 can be subtle.
The difference comes out when the complexity of the task 
reaches a sufficient threshold—GPT-4 is more reliable, 
creative, and able to handle much more 
nuanced instructions than GPT-3.5.

As you can see here, OpenAI claims that the difference between the two models comes down to how you are utilizing the tool. If you are having a ‘casual conversation’, it will have a subtle distinguishment, but when it comes down when the ‘complexity of the task reaches a sufficient threshold’.

Before release, in Beta, GPT-4 was tested in ‘2022–2023 editions of practice exams’ without any prior training to it, and these were the results.

Obviously, GPT-4 failed in the more olden-day subjects, such as Art History and Environmental Science, but apart from them, it excelled in Verbal, as it is a well-trained chatbot, meaning it knows every single technique used in English as it is required for it to be able to emit something humans can understand.

How can I use it?

ChatGPT

OpenAI stated that GPT-4 will be integrated into ChatGPT, meaning you can use it at chat.openai.com without needing to use API requests (see below). It is currently available on ChatGPT Plus, and not out for everyone just yet. To sign up for ChatGPT Plus, visit this site and select Upgrade to Plus in the bottom left corner, above the colour theme selector.

The API

To access GPT-4 via its API, there is a waitlist. For developers, you must join it and wait until keys are given out for you to utilize in your programs and applications. To sign up for the waitlist, vist this site.
